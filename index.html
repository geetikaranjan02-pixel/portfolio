who I am

A brand solutions person who's spent two MBA years getting obsessed with one question: why do people actually choose what they choose? I turn briefs into ideas, and ideas into decks that hold up in a room full of people paid to say no.

what you're probably actually screening for

Not a list of tools. You're looking for someone who can sit in messy, contradictory consumer data and walk out with the one insight worth building a campaign around — and who you'd actually want in the room when the client pushes back.

what's playing while I work

01

Secondary research, first

Before I trust a single instinct, I go read everything that already exists — reports, past campaigns, category trends, competitor moves. I want to know what's already been said so I don't repeat it by accident.

02

Then I go looking for primary proof

Secondary research gives me a hypothesis, not an answer. So I go find real people — surveys, conversations, forums, comment sections — to see if the hypothesis survives contact with actual humans.

03

Paper, before slides

I don't open PowerPoint first. I write the flow of the deck by hand — what's slide 3 arguing, what's the turn on slide 7, where's the one slide that has to land. The paper is where the story gets honest.

04

Only then, the deck

Once the logic is sound on paper, building the actual deck is the easy part. It's just dressing an argument I already trust.
